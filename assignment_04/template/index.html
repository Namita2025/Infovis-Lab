PCA Explained

PCA reduces 13 chemical attributes to 2 dimensions, preserving maximum variance. Each point = one wine sample.

PC1 (36.2%) separates Cultivar 1 (right) from Cultivar 2 (left), driven by alcohol, proline and flavanoids.

PC2 (19.2%) separates Cultivar 3 (top) from the others, driven by color intensity and malic acid.

Glyph encoding:
🎨 Color = cultivar (1/2/3)
▲●■ Shape = KMeans cluster

SPLOM — Chemical Attributes

SPLOM Explained

Each cell shows a scatter plot of two chemical attributes. Color = cultivar. The diagonal labels the attribute.

Use the SPLOM to discover which attributes best separate the 3 cultivars.

Key patterns:
• Flavanoids × Proline: clearest separation of all 3 cultivars
• Alcohol × Proline: Cultivar 1 sits top-right
• Flavanoids × Color intensity: strong negative trend
• Hue × Color intensity: Cultivar 2 clusters separately
